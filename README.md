# **How to Learn Japanese Faster (Applied to This Plan)**

In order to learn Japanese within 90 days, we’re not simply “studying” – we’re **building an architecture of our minds**. The Japanese language is a high context, agglutinative language that follows a subject-object-verb syntax. In order to learn this language in such little time, you have to break away from English-Japanese translation and use **Radical Deconstruction** and **Comprehensible Input**.

---

## 🧠  A Quick Guide: How to Hack Japanese

Before you touch a textbook, you need to understand the three pillars of efficiency for this specific language:

### 1. The SOV Deconstruction
English uses SVO: I (subject) eat (verb) the apple (object).
**The Trick:** Japanese places the action verb at the end of the sentence; everything else in the sentence is just a package of information labeled with a particle. Particles ($wa$, $ga$, $o$, $ni$) are the glue that shows you how to label words in a sentence. If you know the particles, it doesn’t matter where you place your words.

### 2. Radical-Based Kanji (The LEGO Method)
Don't memorize 2,000 individual Kanji. Memorize the **214 Radicals**. 
* **Example:** The Kanji for "Rest" (休) is just "Person" (人) + "Tree" (木). 
* **The Strategy:** Use mnemonics. A person leaning against a tree is *resting*. By learning the building blocks, you decode new characters on sight instead of rote memorization.



### 3. The Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)
60% of daily Japanese conversation consists of just 500-800 words. We will prioritize the **Core 2k Deck** (the 2,000 most common words) via Anki to ensure you aren't wasting time on "encyclopedia" or "astronomy" terms in Week 3.

# 3 months Full Guide 
This is the "Full System" breakdown. To survive this 12-week sprint, you must treat Japanese like a puzzle, not a list of words. We are going to build the **Logic Engine** first, then add the **Vocabulary Fuel**.

---

## 🏗️ Month 1: The Blueprint (Scripts & Phonetics)
*The Goal: Eradicate Romaji (English letters) from your life. If you can’t read the scripts, you aren’t learning Japanese; you’re learning a ghost of it.*

* **Week 1: Hiragana Mastery**
    * **Task:** Learn all 46 basic Hiragana characters.
    * **Method:** Use mnemonics (e.g., "ku" looks like a Cuckoo's beak). 
    * **SRS:** Add 10 characters/day to Anki.
* **Week 2: Katakana & The "Foreign" Ear**
    * **Task:** Learn Katakana. Note that it’s sharper/blockier.
    * **Practice:** Read menus or Japanese brand names. Recognize that "Ko-hi-" is "Coffee."
* **Week 3: The Radical Foundation**
    * **Task:** Learn the first 40 most common Kanji radicals (Water, Fire, Person, Roof, etc.).
    * **Meta-Learning:** Don't learn Kanji meanings yet; learn to *see* the components.
* **Week 4: The Identity Sentence ($A \text{ wa } B \text{ desu}$)**
    * **Grammar:** $A$ (Subject) $+$ は (wa) $+$ $B$ (Noun) $+$ です (desu).
    * **Particles:** Master the Topic Particle (は - wa) and the Question Particle (か - ka).

---

## ⚙️ Month 2: The Framework (The Engine)
*The Goal: Master the "Packet" system. Every Japanese sentence is just a series of info-packets followed by a verb.*

* **Week 5: Action Verbs (The Masu-Form)**
    * **Task:** Learn the "Big 10" verbs: Eat, Drink, Go, Come, See, Do, Buy, Write, Sleep, Speak.
    * **Logic:** All polite verbs end in $~masu$.
* **Week 6: The Object Particle (を - o)**
    * **Grammar:** $S \text{ wa } O \text{ o } V$. ("I apple eat.")
    * **Vocab:** Food, hobbies, and household items.
* **Week 7: The "I-Adjective" Jungle**
    * **Task:** Learn adjectives like *Takai* (Expensive) or *Samui* (Cold).
    * **Conjugation:** Learn how to make them negative ($~kunai$) and past tense ($~katta$).
* **Week 8: The "Na-Adjective" & Locations**
    * **Grammar:** Use に (ni) and へ (e) for movement. "I go to the park."
    * **Kanji:** Start learning N5 level Kanji (Numbers, Days of the week, Directions).

---

## 🌊 Month 3: The Interior (Fluidity & Immersion)
*The Goal: Move from "Static" sentences to "Flowing" Japanese using the Te-form.*

* **Week 9: The Te-Form (The "Glue")**
    * **Crucial Step:** This is the hardest part of beginner Japanese. The Te-form allows you to connect verbs ($V_1 \text{te}, V_2$).
    * **Practice:** "I go to the store AND buy bread."
* **Week 10: State of Being ($~te iru$)**
    * **Grammar:** Present continuous. "I am eating" ($Tabete iru$).
    * **Listening:** Start watching "Japanese with Shun" on YouTube—active listening only.
* **Week 11: Casual vs. Polite (Plain Form)**
    * **Meta-Learning:** Understand that $Taberu$ (Casual) and $Tabemasu$ (Polite) are the same word. Use Plain form for friends, Masu for bosses.
* **Week 12: Review & "The Leap"**
    * **Task:** Focus on particles like も (mo - also), から (kara - from), and まで (made - until).
    * **Immersion:** Attempt to write a 200-character diary entry entirely in Japanese.

## 🧬 Meta-Learning Tactics for Speed

### 1. The "Particle-First" Approach
When you see a long sentence, find the particles first. 
* **は (wa)** = Subject
* **を (o)** = Object
* **に (ni)** = Destination/Time
If you find these, you can translate the sentence backward from the Verb (at the end) to the Subject (at the start).

### 2. Mnemonics for Kanji
Never "draw" a Kanji 100 times. Use stories.
* **Example (雷 - Thunder):** Rain (雨) + Field (田). When it rains on a field, you get thunder.
* **Tool:** Use **Wanikani** or the **RTK (Remembering the Kanji)** method.

### 3. Shadowing (The Vocal Hack)
Don't just read. **Speak.** When you hear a native speaker say a sentence, repeat it immediately, mimicking their pitch and "music." This bypasses the "robotic" accent most learners get.
